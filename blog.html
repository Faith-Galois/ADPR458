Adventures in Cyanotype

6th May, 2024 5:30 pm Faith Galois

This semester I took a Life Drawing class with one of my good friends and we were able to collaborate on our final project. We went back and forth on a lot of different ideas but we finally decided to try cyanotype. My friend had some extra cyanotype papers for printing so we were able to get started right away. Cyanotype works by printing with the sun, so you have to lay what you want to print over the paper and let it sit out in the sun for about 10 minutes. We went out and collected some flowers on campus and found a nice sunny spot to do our "printing." We then took turns being the director of eachothers prints but laying out the pages and positioning the other person and the flowers around the scene. Next, we layed out a cluster of flowers and put our hands on top of them reaching for it. After they are done printing we had to quickly put them back in their black bag so they wouldn't be contaminated by more light. Then we had to rinse the papers off with water to deactivate the chemical that causes the light to darked it the paper. Finally, after they all dried, we both drew eachothers faces in more detail back into the composition using a white charcoal pencil.
